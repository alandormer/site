Dashboard overview

The dashboard is where site activity becomes understandable, reviewable, and reportable.

It brings together recorded events, summaries, and site context so teams can understand what happened, when it happened, and why it mattered — without relying on constant live monitoring.

Live awareness, structured memory

The platform observes activity across camera feeds and converts it into structured, time-stamped events.

This allows operators to maintain awareness during operations, while also building a reliable record that can be reviewed later — minutes, days, or months after the fact.

Events, incidents, and timelines

Noteworthy activity is recorded as factual events rather than isolated alerts.

Incidents can be reviewed as clear timelines, combining video, observations, and context to explain what occurred without relying on fragmented footage or manual reconstruction.

Reporting and handovers

Because activity is structured and stored, reports can be generated automatically in multiple formats.

  • • Incident and investigation reports
  • • Shift and daily handovers
  • • Activity summaries and timelines
  • • Compliance and audit records

Reports are generated directly from recorded events, ensuring consistency and traceability.

Querying site activity

Operators and managers can query past activity to answer specific questions.

This allows teams to look for particular behaviours, time windows, zones, or conditions — without manually reviewing hours of video or relying on memory. Querying complements reporting by enabling focused review when needed.

Context-aware review

Video is reviewed alongside site-specific context, notes, and documentation.

This allows events to be understood in relation to how the site operates, what rules apply, and what is considered normal or expected for that environment.

Designed for integration

The dashboard is designed to sit on top of existing camera systems and site infrastructure.

It supports integrators and operators by adding intelligence, reporting, and oversight capabilities without replacing existing hardware or workflows.

The result is not just visibility, but a durable operational record of how a site actually functions over time.
